The Metrics of Our Analysis Tool


Learn about how we measure success in pilots of our fall/incident analysis & tracking tool to improve safety, save time, and ensure compliance.

What Does Success Look Like?

We're excited to pilot with you and demonstrate the value our tool delivers. Before we begin this pilot, we want to align on what success looks like for your organization with Fallyx's tool.

Quantitative Metrics:

1.) The amount of time saved on incident tracking & auditing:

A 50 - 70% decrease in time spent on incident & fall analysis is a success. This is measured through surveys that we send out before and after the pilot begins which we craft with you.

2.) Reduction in incidents of non-compliance:

This is specific to the type of home (LTC, IL, AL or Memory Care) and how compliance is currently measured in your homes.

Case Study: Independent & Assisted Living

For IL/AL since it is harder to directly measure non-compliance, the main approach we take to evaluate the improvement in non-compliance is through the use of a 3rd-party consultant. We worked with a 124-bed IL/AL home in Ontario and using a consultant the home has already worked with, they evaluated the standard of their incident & falls reporting before and after Fallyx’s implementation based on the RHRA & ORCA guidelines .

They concluded that post-Fallyx’s implementation there was an improvement in falls tracking and increased adherence to compliance according to RHRA & ORCA standards, leading to post-pilot implementation.


Case Study: Long Term Care

A 15 home LTC Chain that uses PCC measures non-compliance with Fallyx’s tool using:

  • – The # of fall incidents where less than 3 post-fall were written in 72hrs.
  • – The # of times there was no fall note in the progress notes section.
    • – # of POAs that were listed as uncontacted by the end of the month.


With Fallyx’s tool they saw a 7% decrease in the above metrics across the 5 homes that were included in the pilot which led to a full-scale implementation of Fallyx’s tool across all 15 of their homes.

3.) Tool Adoption & Engagement

The first question that easily allows us to know if the pilot was suscessful is asking if each community would like to use the tool post-pilot, this is a simple YES or NO answer that gives us a quantative data point.


For engagement we track metrics such as:

  • – Login frequency to Fallyx's dashboards.
  • – Usage of reports in care discussions.
  • – Utilization of graphs for analysis.

We personalize these engagement metrics around the use of Fallyx’s tool with your team based on how you’d like to see it being used. If your staff are consistently logging into our tool and using it to analyze insights from the data, we know that we’ve gathered data which is valuable to your homes.

Qualitative Metrics:

Through this data we're looking to understand the experince your wellness/nursing team has with our tool, as well as providing more insight that can help us drive conclusions. We gather this feedback through surveys including questions such as:


  • – How helpful is the tool in automating monthly fall reports?
  • – On a scale of 1-10, how much time does Fallyx's tool save in manual tracking & analysis?
  • – How effective is the tool in identifying trends and patterns?
  • – Has the data helped prevent future incidents?
